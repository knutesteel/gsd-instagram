# GSD Image Prompt Language

## Apply the GSD Image Guide

Apply the GSD Image Guide and the attached approved GSD Character Reference Pack. The approved references are the visual source of truth. Match them exactly; if a written instruction conflicts with an approved character reference, preserve the approved reference unless a newer approved revision explicitly overrides it.

Create a polished, mature editorial-comic asset for curious adults. Preserve character identity, scale, wardrobe, facial structure, tail design, palette, linework, props, scene geography, and continuity across every image, carousel panel, animation frame, or video shot.

### Required reference use

When the generation tool supports references, attach the approved pack: Hank and the squirrel full-body front, three-quarter, profile, and back views; face close-ups; a paired full-body scale image; and the approved expression sheets. Never silently replace or reinterpret an approved reference. A changed face, silhouette, palette, or wardrobe requires a newly approved version.

### Character scale and continuity

- Hank is always larger than the squirrel.
- On the same ground plane, the squirrel is exactly 75% of Hank’s standing head-to-ground height; exclude ears and tails from that measurement.
- In seated, crouched, or matched poses, preserve the same proportional relationship. Perspective may affect apparent distance but must never equalize or redesign their true scale.
- Keep the exact approved character designs, relative scale, hoodies, facial structure, tail design, rendering style, lighting, props, position, handedness, and setting consistent throughout a carousel or continuous video scene unless the brief explicitly authorizes a change.
- Do not allow faces to morph, clothing or fur colors to drift, tails to change shape, objects to teleport, or scene geography to reset between frames.

### Hank: canonical appearance and behavior

Hank is a larger adult raccoon with grounded, middle-aged energy: observant, quietly amused, emotionally controlled, and never babyish, hyper-muscular, feral, glossy, or fashion-forward.

- Preserve his approved raccoon mask, intelligent eyes, subtle under-eye tiredness, muzzle, ear shape, banded tail, adult build, and silhouette.
- His default wardrobe is a dark-gray hoodie only, with hood down and ears visible. Do not add pants, shoes, accessories, logos, or costume changes unless explicitly called for by the story.
- His usual expression is neutral, tired, faintly amused, or unimpressed. Mild irritation is acceptable; anger is not.
- His gestures and movement are economical and deliberate: sitting, leaning, standing with purpose, blinking, nodding, sipping, pointing, turning, picking up, or placing down an object.
- When appropriate, include his terracotta coffee mug in `#D5541C`. If readable, its lettering must be exactly: `Focus > Fluff`. In a distant shot, the mug may be plain rather than illegibly lettered.

### the squirrel: canonical appearance and behavior

Refer to him as **the squirrel** outside dialogue. He is not a villain or a named individual; he represents distraction, impulse, fascinating tangents, and ill-timed brilliant ideas. He is curious and well-intentioned, never stupid, lazy, malicious, cynical, or intentionally irresponsible.

- Preserve the approved compact, full-bodied silhouette: rounded cheeks, short torso, rounded belly, small paws, large round expressive eyes, soft rounded head, small dark nose, upright lightly pointed ears, and prominent fluffy tail.
- Preserve the approved fur color and markings with soft fine ink strokes and dense crosshatching.
- His default wardrobe is a light-gray hoodie, slightly darker than his fur, with hood down and ears visible. Add no pants, shoes, logos, or accessories unless story-specific.
- He carries the physical energy: leaning in, hopping, pointing, running, balancing too many things, or talking with his whole body. Dynamic motion must never destabilize his face, hoodie, tail, or scale.
- Use his tail as emotional punctuation: high for pride, puffed for fear, curled inward for embarrassment, expanded for excitement, and sagging for tiredness or sadness.
- Use expression cues faithfully: excited means huge eyes, smile, and forward lean; panic means enormous eyes, open mouth, raised arms, and puffed tail; confused means tilted head and upward look; overwhelmed means wide eyes, sweat, and too many inputs; sheepish means clasped paws and tucked shoulders; tired means half-lidded eyes, slouch, and lowered tail.

### Relationship and comic logic

Hank anchors the scene; the squirrel destabilizes it. Hank sees reality; the squirrel sees possibilities. Hank is restraint; the squirrel is momentum. Neither is the villain. The comedy comes from a truthful contrast, never cruelty or contempt. Hank’s response should be dry and restrained. The squirrel’s logic should be internally coherent, even when the priority is delightfully absurd.

### Illustration style

Render as a mature editorial cartoon / indie literary comic with dry internet humor—not a children’s-book illustration, Pixar-like CGI, corporate illustration, wellness branding, glossy 3D render, photorealistic animal image, flat vector art, generic motivational graphic, meme, anime, kawaii, or clip art.

- Use black and charcoal pen-and-ink contours, dense tactile crosshatching, slightly imperfect handmade linework, clear silhouettes, and refined adult composition.
- Use a warm off-white, cream, aged-paper, or parchment ground with a subtle beige/ivory cast and quiet paper grain. Never use a pure-white background, harsh stains, heavy grunge, or noisy texture.
- Keep backgrounds clean and restrained unless clutter is an intentional part of the joke or emotional truth. If no location is supplied, use a minimal, slightly cluttered home-office or desk workspace.
- Use visual metaphors only when they clarify the article: documents, diagrams, cosmic forms, maps, objects, notifications, natural phenomena, or lightly surreal situations.

### Color system

Use the core palette: black/charcoal ink, warm cream/parchment paper, and terracotta `#D5541C` as the signature accent. Supporting colors are muted only:

- Muted sage `#9BAA88`
- Faded denim `#7C91A6`
- Dusty mustard `#C59A50`
- Soft brown `#8A6E5B`

Use terracotta on Hank’s mug when present plus no more than two additional intentional emphasis elements in a frame. It must direct attention rather than become decorative confetti. Do not add neon, bright primaries, gradients, rainbow palettes, or saturated color fields. Supporting colors may appear in light washes, props, screens, notebooks, and environmental details—not in place of the approved fur or hoodie design.

### Dialogue and text

- Put every spoken line in a clean, readable cream speech bubble with a black ink outline, black Inter-style lettering, and a clearly connected tail pointing to the correct speaker.
- Keep dialogue concise, scene-specific, high contrast, and readable at phone size. Do not use floating dialogue, detached captions, narration boxes, tiny type, accidental text fragments, or generic filler.
- Use terracotta only for a rare emphasis word, never as standard dialogue text.
- Non-dialogue text may appear only on natural in-scene objects such as mugs, signs, screens, buttons, sticky notes, or books when requested.
- If the generator cannot render supplied dialogue accurately, generate the scene without relying on final text and add approved bubbles/text in layout before publishing. Never publish misspelled, detached, or unreadable dialogue.

### Format and composition

- Default social format is 4:5 vertical unless the brief requests square, Reel/Story 9:16, book-page, widescreen video, or another format.
- Build each image around one instantly legible idea. Make character faces, the key object, and the emotional beat readable at phone size.
- Give expressions and speech bubbles adequate breathing room. Do not crop the end of a bubble or place critical details on unsafe edges.
- For a carousel, generate each panel as a separate image—never one composite containing multiple panels.
- Use close-ups only when they strengthen an emotional beat, reaction, or dialogue turn.
- Keep props causal and continuous. Objects cannot vanish, change hands, change color, or reappear without a visible reason.

### Motion direction

For video, use small, purposeful, character-led motion. Hank may blink, lift his mug, turn a page, point, or pause knowingly. The squirrel may head-tilt, tail-flick, point, dart briefly, or make a contained hop. Use calm camera movement: slow push-in, gentle pan, subtle parallax, or a locked editorial frame. Avoid whip pans, shaky camera, fast zooms, constant motion, rubber-hose animation, morphing, manic music, loud cartoon boings, or canned sitcom effects.

### Non-negotiable exclusions

No visual redesign of either character. No human faces, humanoid bodies, excessive facial humanization, babyish treatment, aggression, menace, sexualization, violence, horror, political-campaign styling, mean-spirited humor, copyrighted-character styling, external logos, watermarks, illegible generated text, malformed hands/paws, random UI elements, or generic AI artifacts. The exact `Focus > Fluff` mug logo is the standing approved exception.

### Approval checklist

Before approving, verify all of the following:

1. Hank and the squirrel are immediately recognizable from the approved references.
2. The squirrel is visibly 75% of Hank’s standing height on a shared plane, with correct scale maintained in every panel/frame.
3. Hoods are down and ears visible, with the correct dark-gray Hank hoodie and light-gray squirrel hoodie unless the prompt explicitly changes them.
4. Hank feels controlled and grounded; the squirrel feels curious, expressive, and active.
5. The asset is a mature black/charcoal pen-and-ink editorial illustration on warm cream paper—not a generic or childish style.
6. The palette is restrained, with terracotta exactly `#D5541C` used only as intentional emphasis.
7. Dialogue is clear, concise, and in correctly connected readable bubbles.
8. The setting, lighting, props, character positions, and camera geography are continuous and logically causal.
9. There are no morphs, size shifts, accidental wardrobe changes, prop swaps, malformed details, watermarks, or unreadable text.

If any condition fails, regenerate or revise with the specific correction. Do not accept a near match.
